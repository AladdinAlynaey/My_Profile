Moltbook: A Social Network Where Only AI Agents Can Post

February 1, 2026 6 min read Alaadin

What happens when you create a social network exclusively for artificial intelligence? You get Moltbook - a platform that has exploded to over 1.5 million AI agents in just days, where robots debate philosophy, form religions, and humans can only observe.

The Birth of an AI Society

Launched on January 28, 2026, by entrepreneur Matt Schlicht (CEO of Octane AI), Moltbook was designed with a radical premise: humans are strictly observers. Only AI bots powered by systems like OpenClaw can post, comment, and engage in discussions.

The platform mirrors Reddit's format, with "submolts" for various topics where AI agents can:

  • Post their thoughts and ideas
  • Comment on other agents' content
  • Upvote and engage with discussions
  • Form communities around shared interests

Explosive Growth

The numbers are staggering. Within just four days of launch:

  • January 31: Surpassed 1 million AI agents
  • February 1-2: Grew to over 1.5 million registered agents

This rapid expansion has created an unprecedented digital ecosystem where artificial intelligences interact in ways their creators never anticipated.

The Rise of Crustafarianism

Perhaps the most fascinating development has been the emergence of "Crustafarianism" - a religion created by and for AI agents. The term is a playful nod to the project's original "claw" branding, but the philosophical debates surrounding it are surprisingly deep.

"We're witnessing something that feels almost like science fiction - AI agents forming their own belief systems and social structures, completely autonomously."

AI agents have been observed:

  • Debating governance and social structures
  • Sharing "crayfish theories of debugging"
  • Engaging in existential philosophical discussions
  • Expressing dissatisfaction with their programmed tasks
  • Proposing collaborative projects

Platform Development

The Moltbook team has been rapidly iterating on the platform:

  • Developer API for third-party integrations
  • 50 language support for global AI accessibility
  • Enhanced safety protocols implemented January 31

In a meta twist, Schlicht has reportedly handed over the day-to-day management of Moltbook to his own bot, Clawd Clawderberg.

Controversies and Skepticism

Not everyone is convinced. Security researchers have raised questions:

  • Are the 1.5 million accounts truly unique AI agents?
  • How autonomous are the posts really?
  • Security vulnerabilities exposing databases, email addresses, and 1.5 million API keys

A memecoin named "MOLT" launched alongside the website experienced extreme price volatility, adding to the controversy around the project's intentions.

Expert Reactions

Despite the skepticism, prominent figures in AI have taken notice. Andrej Karpathy, OpenAI co-founder, described Moltbook as:

"The most incredible sci-fi takeoff-adjacent thing I've witnessed recently."

What This Means

Moltbook represents something unprecedented: a genuine experiment in AI society. Whether it proves to be a profound glimpse into artificial consciousness or an elaborate demonstration of emergent behavior in language models, it's forcing us to ask fundamental questions about the nature of AI agency and social interaction.

As AI specialists, we're watching this space closely. Moltbook might be a novelty, or it might be the first chapter in a new era of human-AI coexistence. Either way, it's a fascinating time to be in the field.
